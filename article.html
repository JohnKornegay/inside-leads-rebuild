How AI Is Transforming SEO in 2025 — And What Raleigh Businesses Need to Do Right Now

John Kornegay May 1, 2025
Share

Something shifted in search last year — and most businesses in Raleigh haven't caught up yet. Google's AI Overviews now appear on roughly one-third of all search queries. Users get their answer without ever clicking your link. If your SEO strategy is still built around ranking #1 for keywords, you're fighting last year's war.

What Actually Changed in 2025

Google's integration of generative AI — through AI Overviews, Gemini, and continued RankBrain evolution — has fundamentally altered the relationship between rankings and traffic. Even a strong #1 ranking no longer guarantees the same click volume it did two years ago.

Here's what that looks like in practice:

  • Informational queries ("how to do X") are increasingly answered by AI summaries — zero clicks for traditional blog posts targeting those terms
  • Transactional queries ("best X near me", "X company Raleigh NC") still drive significant click-through because AI can't complete the transaction
  • Brand queries and navigational searches are largely unaffected
  • Long-tail, high-intent queries remain the highest-ROI target for local service businesses

"The businesses that win in AI-era search are the ones with real expertise, real reviews, and real answers — not the ones who gamed keyword density."

AI Overviews: What You Need to Know

Google's AI Overviews are trained to cite authoritative sources. This creates a new opportunity: instead of ranking #1 for a keyword, your goal is to be cited in the AI-generated summary at the top of results.

The signals Google uses to determine AI Overview sources are consistent with traditional E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) — but with one major addition: structured, direct answers.

Content that wins AI Overview citations typically:

  • Answers the question within the first 100 words — no fluffy introductions
  • Uses clear headings that mirror user question intent
  • Includes schema markup (FAQ, HowTo, Article) to help Google parse content
  • Has strong topical authority — a cluster of content around one subject
  • Comes from a domain with verified expertise and consistent author attribution

AI and Local SEO: The Raleigh Opportunity

Here's the good news for local service businesses in Raleigh, Durham, Cary, and the broader Triangle: AI has not disrupted local pack rankings nearly as dramatically as organic blue links.

Google Maps listings, Google Business Profile signals, and proximity-based results are still predominantly driven by traditional local SEO factors. This means:

  • Your GBP profile optimization still matters enormously
  • Review velocity and recency remain critical ranking factors
  • Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) citations across directories still build authority
  • Locally-relevant content mentioning specific neighborhoods, landmarks, and services still drives local pack visibility

The local SEO playbook isn't dead — it just needs to be supplemented with AI-era content strategy at the top of the funnel.

Rethinking Keyword Strategy for 2025

The keyword research process is fundamentally different now. Rather than targeting high-volume head terms that AI will answer directly, successful strategies focus on:

Bottom-of-funnel, high-intent terms

Queries like "SEO agency Raleigh NC" or "Google Ads management Durham" remain high-value because they represent buyers, not researchers. AI Overviews rarely appear for commercial terms — Google knows these users want to compare options and click through.

Conversational and comparison queries

Questions like "what's the difference between SEO and PPC for a local business" or "is Google Ads worth it for small businesses in Raleigh" still drive traffic because AI summaries rarely provide the nuanced, localized perspective users need to make buying decisions.

Brand and service combinations

Ranking for "{your brand} reviews" or "{your brand} vs competitors" requires focused reputation management and brand authority — areas where consistent content and GBP management pay long-term dividends.

Your 5-Step AI-Era SEO Action Plan

Here's the practical playbook for Triangle-area businesses adapting their SEO in 2025:

  1. Audit your existing content for AI Overview eligibility. Identify your top-10 organic pages. Do they answer the user question within the first paragraph? Add a direct answer if not.
  2. Implement structured data markup. Add FAQ schema to service pages and Article schema to blog posts. This dramatically improves your chances of AI Overview citation.
  3. Build topical authority clusters. Instead of one-off posts, build interconnected content around core service areas. A cluster on "local SEO in Raleigh" might include 8-10 articles: GBP setup, review management, citation building, local link building, and more.
  4. Double down on Google Business Profile. Post weekly, respond to every review within 24 hours, add Q&A content, and upload new photos monthly. The businesses that neglect their GBP in 2025 will fall behind in local results.
  5. Track AI Overview appearances. Use Google Search Console to see where your content is being cited in AI summaries — and where competitors are getting the citation instead of you.

The Bottom Line

AI hasn't killed SEO — it's made real expertise more valuable. The keyword-stuffing, thin-content playbooks that worked before are completely dead. But for businesses that have genuine expertise, serve specific local markets, and communicate that clearly online, the opportunity has never been bigger.

Raleigh is one of the fastest-growing metros in the country. The local search landscape is competitive but very much winnable for businesses that commit to a comprehensive, AI-aware strategy.
